Designing a web app for the International Island Film Festival of Groix (FIFIG)

A UI case study

In 2001, a bunch cinema lovers of very small Island, created the first International Island Film Festival of Groix (FIFIG for the close friends). Their goal: facilitate relationship, through an event, by giving voice to islanders around the world.

I thought that the FIFIG would be a great topic for my web design project as I could see areas for improvement in its user experience as well.

I’m sure you want to discover my idea for the new website.

But before that let’s set the scene !

Remember last summer ?

When you tried so badly to reach the next festival event checked on your paper program. And you can’t find your programm. And I’m sure it is already unreadable covered by a large amount of beer stains and something else that we prefer not to know.

I think I have a solution.

The first investigation

First I went on the website and navigated through it to better understand the architecture information. The actual website was very dense, with plenty of information. Perhaps too much ?

The site map

Based on this observation, I decided to reorganize the content of the website, to create a simpler navigation, and to bring a bit more of the festival essence to the website.

I wanted a website super simple and stupid that festival goer can rely on before, but above all during the festival.

Indeed I must say, that sometimes I was a bit overwhelmed with the amount of things to see, and watch, and listen, and do and the lack of digital solution to register my selection and help me organize my time.

The wireframes

Then I worked on the low-fi wireframes to get a clearer idea of what I was looking for.

The moodboard

The next step for me was to define a new visual identity, while staying true to the values of FIFIG.

To get the right global “mood” about the FIFIG, I conducted 6 interviews with former participants and asked them to describe their own experience.

Here is what they said :

  1. Polymorph since you can watch a movie, listen to a concert, join a play.
  2. Family centered as kids are living their best lives in total freedom.
  3. Playful as festival goers can test new things everyday.
  4. Warm since people are all on vacation looking at their best.
  5. Friendly because they always live the place with a bunch of new friends.

The style tile

I wanted also go for a flat graphic style. No shadows, or gradients.

For the colors I wanted that corail color which is such a welcoming tone. I mix it with bright colours. Yellow for the summer. A bit of blue for the sea surrounding the island. And a bit of white to remain the festival goers the typical houses of Groix.

Roboto was a natural choice as a discreet typeface. So it will not steal the stage from the posters and pictures.

I also choose to use super simple icons.

The hi-fi wireframes for a responsive website

The mobile version

That’s why, when designing the new website I decided to focus on the mobile version first. To finally get rid off the paper program ;) ! Let’s save the planet !

The desktop version

Then I adapted the mobile version to a desktop version.

The desirability testing

I conducted 15 desirability tests & 5 usability tests.

And I’m glad that the desirability test were very much in line with the brand attribute. Family oriented / cheerful / young / cool were the 4 most selected words among 42.

Thanks to test and feedbacks I managed to make the website more and more appealing and closer to the spirit I wanted to have by making it “rounder” and “colorful” to better convey the sincerity and excitement of the festival.

The key learnings

Working with short feedback loops and intense collaboration with festival goers made the progress very much apparent at the end of each iteration. It ensured that assumptions would become certainties quickly and made "pivoting" a natural part of the process.

100 % loved the new fresh design.

And loved the possibility to record the events in which they want to participate on a personal programme.

Couple of them (actually the eldery) ask me to improve the detailed programme display to be less confusing.

Finally it was very convenient to me to talk to user & test as quickly as possible at every stage of my project.

The next steps

The lovely testers came up with some great ideas that I would to share with you.

They suggested to :

  1. Add a filter based on the personality of our festival goers (early bird, night owl). To help them to plan their day based on their chronobiological rhythm.
  2. Create a new feature allowing them to synchronize their FIFIG programme selection with their mobile calendar. They could set up reminders to leave the beach on time.
  3. Implement a geolocation system to allow them to see how far they are from all the events scheduled. And never missed one again.

Thanks for stopping by! I hope you enjoyed the read.
